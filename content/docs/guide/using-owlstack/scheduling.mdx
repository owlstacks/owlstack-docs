# Scheduling Posts

Schedule your posts in advance so your content goes live at the best times — even when you're away.

## How to Schedule a Post

1. Create a new post (or edit a draft)
2. Instead of clicking **Publish Now**, click **Schedule**
3. Pick a date and time
4. Select your time zone
5. Click **Confirm Schedule**

Your post will be published automatically at the scheduled time.

## Content Calendar

The **Calendar** view gives you a visual overview of:

- **Scheduled posts** — upcoming posts waiting to be published
- **Published posts** — past posts that have gone live
- **Drafts** — unfinished posts you can schedule later

You can drag and drop posts on the calendar to reschedule them.

## Best Practices

### When to Post

The best posting times depend on your audience, but here are general guidelines:

| Platform | Best Times (Your Audience's Time Zone) |
|:---------|:--------------------------------------|
| Instagram | 11 AM – 1 PM, 7 PM – 9 PM |
| Twitter / X | 8 AM – 10 AM, 12 PM – 1 PM |
| LinkedIn | 7 AM – 8 AM, 12 PM, 5 PM – 6 PM |
| Facebook | 1 PM – 4 PM |
| Pinterest | 8 PM – 11 PM |

### Consistency

Regular posting keeps your audience engaged. Use the calendar to plan a consistent schedule — for example, 3 posts per week spread across different days.

### Bulk Scheduling

Need to schedule many posts at once? Use the **Bulk Schedule** feature to upload multiple posts and set their publishing times all at once.

## Editing Scheduled Posts

1. Go to the **Calendar** or **Posts** section
2. Click on a scheduled post
3. Edit the content, platforms, or schedule time
4. Save your changes

## Canceling a Scheduled Post

Click on the scheduled post and select **Cancel Schedule**. The post will be moved back to drafts.
